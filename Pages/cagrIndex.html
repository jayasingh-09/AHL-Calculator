What is CAGR?

CAGR represents the average annual growth rate of an investment over a specified period, assuming consistent reinvestment of returns. Unlike absolute returns, CAGR accounts for the time factor, offering a better understanding of your financial progress.

How to Calculate CAGR?

The CAGR formula is:

CAGR = (FV / PV) ^ (1 / n) – 1

Where:

FV: Future Value
PV: Present Value
n: Time Period in Years

For example:

If you invested ₹1,00,000 (PV) and its value grew to ₹10,00,000 (FV) in 5 years (n), the CAGR would be:

CAGR = (10,00,000 / 1,00,000) ^ (1 / 5) – 1 = 0.589 or 58.9% annually.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is CAGR return?

+

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) is a financial metric used to measure the average annual growth rate of an investment over a specified time period, assuming the profits are reinvested at the end of each period. It provides a smoothed rate of return, eliminating the effects of market volatility, and is widely used to compare the performance of investments.

Does CAGR reflect investment risks?

+

No, CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) does not reflect investment risks. While CAGR provides a clear and simplified measure of average annual growth over a specified period, it does not account for the fluctuations, volatility, or uncertainty that occurred during that time. This limitation makes it essential to consider other metrics and factors alongside CAGR when evaluating an investment.

Is calculating CAGR a better idea than working on IRR?

+

While CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) and IRR (Internal Rate of Return) both measure investment performance, CAGR can often be a more reliable indicator of overall financial health and stability under specific circumstances.
