Description

CAGR, or Compound Annual Growth Rate, is a crucial metric for assessing business performance and investment returns. It measures the average annual growth rate of an investment over a specific period, considering the impact of compounding.

While there are numerous online CAGR calculators, choosing a user-friendly and accurate tool like Ashiana Housing's calculator can significantly simplify the process.

How Can a CAGR Calculator Help You?

A CAGR calculator offers several benefits:

  • Evaluate Business Performance:** It helps assess your business's growth trajectory and identify areas for improvement.
  • Compare Investment Options:** By calculating the CAGR of different investments, you can make informed decisions about where to allocate your funds.
  • Measure Relative Performance:** Compare your business's growth to industry benchmarks or competitors.

How to Calculate CAGR?

The CAGR formula is:

= (FV / PV)^(1/n) - 1

Where:

Term Definition
FV Future Value
PV Present Value
n Number of years

Example of CAGR Calculation

Let's say you invested ₹1 lakh (PV) and it grew to ₹10 lakh (FV) over 5 years (n). The CAGR would be:

= (10,00,000 / 1,00,000)^(1/5) - 1

This calculates to a CAGR of approximately 58.9%.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is CAGR?

+

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) represents the annual growth rate of an investment, assuming it grows at a consistent rate over the specified period.

How is CAGR different from absolute return?

+

Absolute return only shows the total growth, while CAGR accounts for the time value of money, reflecting yearly returns.

What is the formula for CAGR?

+

CAGR = [(Ending Value / Beginning Value) ^ (1 / Number of Years)] - 1.

Why is CAGR important?

+

CAGR helps investors compare investment performance over time and against benchmarks, providing a clearer growth perspective.
