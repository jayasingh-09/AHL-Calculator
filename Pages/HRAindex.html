What is an HRA Calculator?

As the cost of living in many Indian cities continues to rise, many organizations provide House Rent Allowance (HRA) as part of their employees' salaries. The HRA calculator can help you determine the amount of HRA you are eligible for based on your salary, the rent you pay, and the city you live in.

The HRA slabs in India have been categorized into three distinct groups after the 7th Pay Commission recommendations:

  • Slab X: Most urban cities where the cost of living is high.
  • Slab Y: Marginally lower-cost cities.
  • Slab Z: Smaller cities with relatively lower living costs.

How Can the HRA Calculator Help You?

The HRA exemption calculator is a useful tool for salaried employees who receive HRA as part of their salary. It helps calculate the tax-exempt portion of the HRA based on various factors. To claim HRA exemptions, the following conditions must be met:

  • Salaried Status: You must be a salaried individual receiving HRA.
  • Rent Paid: You should be living in a rented accommodation.
  • Exceeds 10% of Salary: The HRA must exceed 10% of your basic salary.

Using our HRA tax exemption calculator, you can determine how much tax you need to pay and how much of your HRA can be claimed as an exemption.

How to Calculate HRA in India?

Groww’s HRA calculation tool follows a standard formula to determine the House Rent Allowance payable. The salary components considered for the calculation include Basic Salary, Dearness Allowance, and other variable commissions. The HRA tax exemption is calculated based on the lowest of the following three heads:

  • Actual Rent Paid – 10% of Basic Salary
  • HRA Provided by Employer
  • 40%-50% of Basic Salary, depending on the city.

Example of HRA Calculation

Let’s take a fictional example of Person A, who lives in Delhi and pays ₹12,000 in rent every month. Here’s his salary breakdown:

Salary component Total amount (each month)
Basic Salary ₹23,000
HRA ₹15,000
Conveyance Allowance ₹3,000
Medical Allowance ₹1,250
Special Allowance ₹2,300

Adding the components, we identify that Individual A has a monthly salary of ₹44,500.

Now, using the HRA exemption calculator, the figures are calculated as follows:

  • Actual Rent Paid – 10% of Basic: ₹12,000 – ₹2,300 = ₹9,700
  • HRA Provided by Employer: ₹15,000
  • 50% of Basic Salary: ₹11,500

The lowest amount among these three is ₹9,700, so Person A can claim ₹9,700 as HRA exemption from his taxable income.

Key HRA Calculation Terms

  • HRA Tax Exemption: The amount of HRA that can be claimed as exempt from income tax under Section 10(13A).
  • HRA Formula: The standard formula used for calculating HRA based on various components.
  • HRA Percentage: The percentage of Basic Salary allocated as HRA by the employer.
  • HRA Deduction: The deduction of HRA from your taxable income based on the applicable exemptions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which documents prove I have paid my rent?

+

Receipts of the rent paid during the last financial year are applicable. If you do not have the receipts, you will have to show financial statements proving you have paid rent.

Who is eligible to use the HRA Calculator?

+

Salaried employees receiving HRA as part of their salary structure and paying rent are eligible to use the HRA Calculator.

Can self-employed individuals claim HRA?

+

No, HRA is a benefit exclusively for salaried employees. However, self-employed individuals can claim rent deductions under Section 80GG of the Income Tax Act.
