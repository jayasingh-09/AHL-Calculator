Description

If you're an employee of a private company in India, you're eligible to receive gratuity under the Payment of Gratuity Act 1972 after completing five years of service. However, in certain circumstances such as injury or disability due to an accident or disease, you may be eligible for gratuity even before five years. The gratuity amount is calculated based on your last drawn salary, including basic salary and Dearness Allowance (DA), and the number of years you have worked in the company.

Gratuity Calculation Formula

The gratuity calculation formula used by Ashiana Housing’s online tool is:

G = n × b × 15 / 26

Where:

n Number of years of service
b Last drawn basic salary + Dearness Allowance (DA)
15 Constant factor
26 Total working days in a month
G Gratuity amount

For instance, if you’ve worked for 5 years with a monthly salary (including DA) of Rs. 30,000, the gratuity amount would be:

G = 5 × 30,000 × 15 / 26 = Rs. 86,538.46

Key Factors of Gratuity

  • Your gratuity cannot exceed Rs. 10 lakh, with any amount above this considered ex gratia.
  • For work periods beyond 5 years, fractions like 17 years and 6 months will be rounded off to 18 years.

Eligibility for Gratuity

To be eligible for gratuity, you must meet the following criteria under the Payment of Gratuity Act 1972:

  • You should have completed at least 5 years of service with your employer.
  • You are eligible for gratuity if you're near the age of superannuation, have worked continuously, and have no other full-time employer.

If you meet these criteria, use our free online calculator to determine how much gratuity you are entitled to.

Gratuity Taxation

The taxation on gratuity depends on the type of employee:

  • Government employees: Gratuity is exempt from tax.
  • Private-sector employees: The tax-exempt gratuity amount depends on several factors, with a lifetime limit of Rs. 20 lakh.
